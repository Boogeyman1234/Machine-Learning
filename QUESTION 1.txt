QUESTION 1
Business Interpretation Report
Objective

The objective of this project was to segment mall customers based on their annual income and spending behavior to help the retail chain design targeted marketing campaigns.

Methodology

Data preprocessing included checking missing values, duplicate records, and feature selection. Annual Income and Spending Score were selected as clustering features. StandardScaler was applied to normalize the data before clustering.

The Elbow Method was used to determine the optimal number of clusters. K-Means clustering was then applied with K = 5. Model quality was evaluated using the Silhouette Score.

Results

The clustering model achieved a Silhouette Score of 0.555, indicating good separation between customer groups.

Five customer segments were identified:

Average Customers
Premium Customers
Impulsive Shoppers
Careful Wealthy Customers
Budget Customers

Each segment demonstrated unique spending characteristics and purchasing behavior.

Business Impact

Customer segmentation allows the company to personalize marketing campaigns instead of using a single strategy for all customers.

This can improve:

Customer retention
Marketing efficiency
Revenue generation
Customer satisfaction

Final Recommendation and Decision-Making Summary
Based on the K-Means clustering analysis, customers were divided into five distinct segments according to their Annual Income and Spending Score. The retail chain should adopt different marketing strategies for each customer group:

1. Premium Customers (High Income, High Spending)
Provide VIP memberships and loyalty rewards.
Offer premium and luxury products.
Give early access to sales and exclusive events.
Focus on customer retention.
2. Average Customers (Medium Income, Medium Spending)
Offer seasonal discounts and promotional campaigns.
Encourage membership enrollment.
Recommend complementary products to increase spending.
3. Impulsive Shoppers (Low Income, High Spending)
Provide bundle offers and cashback rewards.
Use personalized promotions to maintain engagement.
Monitor spending behavior and target frequently purchased products.
4. Careful Wealthy Customers (High Income, Low Spending)
Send personalized recommendations.
Introduce premium products through targeted marketing.
Offer exclusive discounts to encourage purchases.
5. Budget Customers (Low Income, Low Spending)
Focus on affordable products.
Provide discount coupons and value packs.
Run price-sensitive promotional campaigns.
Decision-Making Summary

The clustering model successfully identified five meaningful customer groups with a Silhouette Score of 0.555, indicating good cluster separation.

The retail chain should implement a customer-segmentation-based marketing strategy rather than using the same promotional approach for all customers. By targeting each customer segment with personalized offers and campaigns, the company can maximize revenue, improve customer experience, and make more informed business decisions.
